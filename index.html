Int. J. Heat Mass Transfer 2022

Deep Reinforcement Learning for Heat Exchanger Shape Optimization

H. Keramati, F. Hamdullahpur, M. Barzegari

A parametric approach for heat exchanger shape optimization utilizing Deep Reinforcement Learning and Boundary Representation (BREP). Continuous geometric representation of the fluid and solid domain facilitates boundary condition implementation and design space exploration. The framework consists of a Deep Neural Network, a CFD solver with automatic body-fitted mesh generation to solve single fin shape optimization. The RL agent explores the design space using Proximal Policy Optimization in combination with a FEniCS-based CFD environment. The method alleviates the curse of dimensionality compared to voxel and pixel-based optimization of coupled thermal fluid-structure problems.

Eng. Appl. Artif. Intell. 2026

A Reward-Directed Diffusion Framework for Generative Design

H. Keramati, P. Kirchen, M. Hannan, R. K. Jaiman

A generative optimization framework built on a fine-tuned diffusion model and reward-directed sampling to generate high-performance engineering designs. The framework employs parametric representations to generate new parameter sets corresponding to designs with improved aerodynamic and hydrodynamic performance. By using an entropy-regularized approach, this work introduces the iterative use of a soft value function within a Markov decision process framework to achieve reward-guided decoding. The proposed approach distributes computational and memory costs across training and inference phases, facilitating generation of high-reward designs even beyond the training data.

Int. J. Heat Mass Transfer 2026

HeatGen: A Guided Diffusion Framework for Multiphysics Heat Sink Design Optimization

H. Keramati, M. Sadeghi, R. K. Jaiman

A generative optimization framework based on a guided denoising diffusion probabilistic model that leverages surrogate gradients to generate heat sink designs minimizing pressure drop while maintaining surface temperatures below a specified threshold. Geometries are represented using boundary representations of multiple fins, and a multi-fidelity approach is employed to generate training data. Two residual neural networks predict pressure drop and surface temperature, whose gradients guide geometry generation toward satisfying low-pressure and temperature constraints. Once trained, inference under new constraints is computationally inexpensive and does not require retraining.
